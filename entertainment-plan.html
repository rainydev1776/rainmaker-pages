Auger's streams

Rainmaker account Thu May 7, 2025; May stream nights May 9, 10, 12, 15, 16, 18, 22, 23, 24, 27, 29, 30, 31 (first public Sat May 9)—heavy college/NBA slates.

Auger streams are the daily live spine for pulling demand out of crypto-only circles: proof people can watch in real time. Stand up the Rainmaker account and access by Thursday May 7, 2025 so overlays and CTAs are not last-minute. May 2025 stream calendar (all Rainmaker live nights): May 9, 10, 12, 15, 16, 18, 22, 23, 24, 27, 29, 30, 31—first public stream Saturday May 9; stack each night on dense college and NBA games where the schedule allows. Between those dates, Rainmaker still shows up when betting attention is highest—picks, payouts, first-win path—between Spike drops and Kate’s Tue/Fri posts. Rebrand overlays and BRB screens match the Friday May 8, 2025 rebrand complete date so stream and street stay on one system. The stream is both the sermon and the raw tape for Dan and short-form.

X influencers (Kate and peers)

Credible voices in timeline culture—simple talking points, proof assets, one URL.

Reference: x.com/_kate_lv

X (Kate and peers) is timeline culture: where sports and betting arguments already happen, outside project-only echo chambers. Micro- and mid-tier X creators turn Rainmaker into a daily conversation in timelines that sports and betting culture already lives in. The lane needs credible voices, consistent posting, and audience overlap with the product. Packages should be simple—talking points, proof assets, and a single URL—so each post compounds instead of feeling like a one-off ad drop.

Wildposting

Street-visible brand in the right neighborhoods; QR bridges to the same funnel as digital.

Site: wildposting.com

Wildposting carries proof to the physical city—people who never touch Crypto Twitter still walk past the poster. Wildposting is the street layer: wheatpaste and OOH in the right neighborhoods and nights-out corridors so the brand exists in the physical city, not only in feeds. Creative stays bold and legible; QR and URL bridge to the same funnel as digital. Cadence and city rotation matter more than a single poster moment—people should bump into Rainmaker more than once on their way to the game or the bar.

↑ Back to Phase 0: Retail

Dan clipping Auger (IG, TikTok, Reels, X)

Turn long live proof into short hits—native cut and caption per platform.

Dan clips are how stream proof wins the scroll-native mainstream—short hits that travel faster than a live URL alone. Dan (and the clip network) reframes long live moments into short hits: the streak, the cashout, the call, the reaction. Each clip should carry a consistent visual hook and a path to sign up or follow. IG, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and X each need native framing—same core clip, different caption and length—so distribution does not cannibalize itself.

↑ Back to Phase 0: Retail

Rainmaker theme song

Audio recall tied to the same proof moments—not a disconnected pop launch.

The theme song is audio recall between clips and streams—something people can Shazam, hum, and hear without rereading a thread. Release it with stems and clean hooks for creators and editors. Pair the drop with the social challenge and clip cadence so the track rides the same proof moments as the product, not a disconnected pop launch.

↑ Back to Phase 0: Retail

Email list

Owned reach—proof, launches, and invites without an algorithm deciding who sees it.

Email is owned retail comms—no feed algorithm between you and people who already raised their hand. The email list is the owned lane: people who already raised their hand (waitlist, sign-ups, event captures). Use it for nightly or weekly proof, product milestones, and later the same spine for music drops—one subject line, one CTA, creative that matches the stream and clip story. Keep consent, frequency, and unsubscribe clean so it stays a trust channel, not spam; segment when you have enough signal (e.g. traders vs. listeners-only) so messages stay relevant.

↑ Back to Phase 0: Retail

Debit card plan

Spend and status in the real world—after someone already believes the model.

Debit is the physical-world chapter once someone already believes the digital retail story—wallet presence, not just screenshots. The card is spend and status in the real world: Rainmaker in wallet, tap-to-pay story, and a tangible reason to stay funded in the ecosystem. Position it as the next chapter after someone already believes the model—onboarding, compliance, and waitlist narrative should match the rest of the sheet. Retail, music, and Auger streams all point toward “this is serious enough for plastic in your hand.”
