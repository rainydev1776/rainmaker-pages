How It Works

Three systems running behind the scenes. The user just sees their balance go up.

1

Smart Stake

Every trade is automatically 10% of their balance. No decisions. Win → balance grows → stake grows. Loss only hits 10%.

2

Recovery Shield

User loses? We issue locked RAIN credits from our treasury to cover it. Their balance looks unchanged. Next trade is the same size. Credit repays itself over 2-3 wins.

3

Circuit Breaker

If the model goes cold (regime change, play-ins), we auto-cut stake at 15% drawdown and pause at 25%. Prevents cascading losses.

How the Shield costs us nothing: We issue RAIN from our treasury as a locked credit. The user's idle deposits (90% of their balance) provide the USDC float. Credits repay in ~48 hours via future wins. RAIN returns to treasury. Net cost = $0.

RAIN Buyback

5% of every retail user's profit auto-buys RAIN. It's locked until they hit 500K — then fees drop and buyback stops. They can't sell it, so there's only buy pressure.

Under 500K RAIN

5% of profit auto-buys RAIN every win. Locked — can't sell, withdraw, or transfer. Accumulates until 500K.

Hits 500K → Bronze

Platform fee drops from 22% to 15%. Buyback stops. RAIN unlocks. User keeps more profit.

Why they hold

Selling below 500K = back to 22% fees + 5% buyback. The incentive to hold is stronger than the incentive to sell.

Total RAIN buy pressure: $900K+ over 5.5 months. Only buy pressure — no sell pressure until Bronze. New users constantly replace promoted users' buying.
