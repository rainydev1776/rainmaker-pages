What Is AutoPilot?

Three systems that remove every decision from the user. They deposit, flip the switch, and walk away.

1

Smart Stake

What

Every trade is automatically sized at 10% of the user's balance. No manual input.

Why

Prevents emotional sizing. A loss only hits 10% of the balance. Recoverable in ~1 winning trade.

Example

$10,000 balance → $1,000 per trade. Win → balance grows → stake grows. Proportional. Automatic.

2

Recovery Shield

What

When a user loses, the platform issues them locked RAIN credits equal to the loss. Their balance looks unchanged.

Why

Compounding never breaks. The user's next trade is the same size as if the loss never happened.

Example

User loses $1,000 → receives $1,000 in locked RAIN → next trade still stakes $1,000 → credit repays over 2-3 wins.

3

Circuit Breaker

What

If a user's balance drops 15% from its peak, stake cuts to 5%. At 25% drop, trading pauses 24h.

Why

Catches regime changes (NBA play-ins, model recalibration) before they cause cascading losses.

Example

Model has 2 losses in a row → breaker cuts stake in half → 3rd loss does half damage → model recalibrates → auto-resumes.

Step by step, what happens when a Pro user loses a trade.

User loses $1,000
Burst 2.0 trade goes wrong. 10% stake is lost.
2

This RAIN is deposited into the user's account as a LOCKED credit. They cannot withdraw or sell it.
3
User's dashboard still shows $10,000 effective balance
Under the hood: $9,000 real USDC + $1,000 in locked RAIN credit. But the user sees $10,000.
4

Getting a Shield credit can actually push a user closer to their next fee discount tier. Losing a trade = closer to Bronze.
5

Win $960 gross → $672 net after fees → $336 goes to repayment (buys RAIN back for treasury) → $336 to user.
7
1-2 more wins → credit fully repaid → RAIN returns to treasury
Treasury is whole again. User's balance is all real USDC. Compounding resumes at full speed. Total time with credit: ~48 hours.

RAIN Auto-Buyback

5% of every retail user's profit automatically buys RAIN. That RAIN is LOCKED in their account until they hit 500K — the first fee discount tier. They cannot sell it, withdraw it, or move it.

How It Works — Step by Step

Every time a retail user (someone who doesn't yet hold 500K RAIN) wins a trade, this happens automatically:

1
User wins a trade — gross profit: $960
Burst 2.0 fires. $1,000 stake returns $1,960. Gross profit = $960.
2
Fees are calculated on the $960 profit
22% platform fee ($211) + 5% RAIN buyback ($48) + 3% Shield ($29) = $288 total fees. User keeps $672.
3
The 5% ($48) immediately buys RAIN at market price
This happens automatically on every winning trade settlement. The user doesn't click anything. It just happens.
4
The purchased RAIN is deposited into the user's account — LOCKED
The user can see their RAIN balance growing on their dashboard. But they CANNOT sell it, withdraw it, or transfer it. It is locked.
5
RAIN keeps accumulating trade after trade
Every winning trade adds more locked RAIN. The balance grows daily. The user watches it approach the 500K threshold.
6
User hits 500K RAIN → auto-promotes to Bronze tier
The moment their locked RAIN hits 500K, they are promoted. Platform fee drops from 22% to 15%. The 5% buyback STOPS — they no longer pay it.
7
RAIN unlocks at Bronze
Once promoted, their RAIN is unlocked. They can hold it (to maintain Bronze), sell some, or keep accumulating toward Silver (3M) and Gold (6M).

Why Locking Matters

Without locking

User receives RAIN → immediately sells it → no price impact → token goes nowhere → tier system is meaningless.

With locking

User receives RAIN → locked until 500K → only buy pressure on the market → price goes up → reaching 500K costs less in dollar terms over time → more users can afford tiers.

After unlocking (Bronze+)

User CAN sell — but won't. Selling below 500K means going back to 22% fees + 5% mandatory buyback. The economic incentive to hold is stronger than the incentive to sell.

Bottom Line

Rainmaker puts in $0 USD. The RAIN treasury backs the credits — RAIN goes out, RAIN comes back within 48 hours. The actual USDC for trades comes from the 90% of user deposits sitting idle.
